About Me

I am a first year PhD student advised by Dr. Martine De Cock in the School of Engineering and Technology at the University of Washington Tacoma. I am a Graduate Research Assistant in the Privacy-preserving Machine learning group led by Dr. Martine De Cock and Dr. Anderson Nascimento.

I am broadly interested in Trustworthy Machine Learning. Currently, my research focuses on privacy-preserving machine learning and fairness in machine learning.

I recieved my Masters in Computer Science & Systems from University of Washington Tacoma in 2020 with a thesis on privacy-preserving video classification that was nominated for the UW Distinguished Thesis award. Prior to starting at UW Tacoma, I spent a few years in the Nuclear Power Generation industry as an IT administrator after recieving my Bachelors from JawaharLal Nehru Technological University Anantapur in 2009.

When I am not working, I love to play with my 3 year old daughter. I love dancing and like watching movies of different genres.
