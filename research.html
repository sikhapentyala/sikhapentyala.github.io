Research Overview

My research focuses on the overarching domain of Responsible and Trustworthy AI. A central question guiding my work: How can we design AI systems while safeguarding the privacy of individuals? I develop principled methods at the intersection of privacy, synthetic data generation, fairness, and interpretability — with real-world applications in healthcare, genomics, and finance. My work combines theoretical guarantees (differential privacy, secure multiparty computation) with practical systems deployed on national infrastructure such as the NAIRR.

Research Areas

1. Privacy-Preserving Synthetic Data Generation

Synthetic data generation (SDG) produces artificial replicas of real data that preserve statistical properties without exposing private records — enabling open research in regulated domains. My work addresses a critical gap: how to generate synthetic data from distributed data silos that no single party is permitted to access in full. I combine Differential Privacy (DP) and Secure Multiparty Computation (MPC) to build the first systems capable of this. Current focus areas include privacy vulnerabilities in existing SDG methods, synthetic genomic and single-cell RNA-seq data generation on the NAIRR, and extending SDG to multi-modal and longitudinal data.

Differential Privacy Secure MPC Generative Models Genomics scRNA-seq NAIRR

Key papers: CaPS (ICML 2024), IEEE SaTML 2025, PPAI-2025, GenoPri'24, SyntheticData4ML@NeurIPS 2022

2. Federated Learning & Privacy-Preserving AI

Federated learning enables multiple parties to collaboratively train ML models without sharing raw data. My work extends FL to handle sensitive settings where even intermediate model updates must remain private. I develop MPC protocols for secure aggregation, privacy-preserving inference (video classification, anomaly detection), and collaborative training across vertical and horizontal data splits. Notable work includes the U.S.-U.K. PETs Prize Challenge (2nd prize, financial crime detection), the iDASH 2021 genome analysis competition (1st place), and the NIST Privacy-Preserving Federated Learning Blog Series.

Federated Learning MPC Differential Privacy Financial AI Healthcare

Key papers: ICML 2021, PoPETS 2024, PLOS ONE 2024, U.S.-U.K. PETs Prize Challenge, iDASH 2021

3. Fairness & Explainability in AI

Privacy cannot be addressed in isolation — AI systems must also be fair and explainable. My work at the intersection of privacy and fairness includes: MPC protocols for bias auditing (PrivFair), privacy-preserving group fairness in federated learning, and fair item ranking under user privacy constraints. At JPMorgan Chase, I developed differentially private algorithmic recourse — giving actionable feedback to individuals affected by automated decisions while protecting the privacy of the training population (US Patent 18/517,211). I also designed novel interpretable architectures for time-series models (pending patent).
